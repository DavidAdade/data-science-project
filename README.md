# Sports Data Analytics & Python for Athletes
## 6-Week Curriculum Overview for Stakeholders

---

### **Program Summary**

**Duration:** 6 weeks | 12 sessions (90 minutes each, twice weekly)  
**Target Audience:** High school athletes with zero coding experience  
**Location:** Ontario, Canada  
**Platform:** Google Colab (free, browser-based, no software installation required)  
**Philosophy:** Sports first, code second – every concept connects to athletic performance

---

### **Program Goals**

By the end of this program, students will be able to:
- Analyze professional and personal sports performance using data
- Write basic Python code to load, manipulate, and visualize datasets
- Create data-driven insights using real NBA, NHL, and MLS statistics
- Present analytical findings like professional sports analysts
- Understand career pathways in sports technology and analytics

---

## **Week-by-Week Breakdown**

### **Week 1: Introduction to Data & Coding**
**Sessions 1-2 | Foundation Building**

**What Students Learn:**
- What sports analytics is and why professional teams invest millions in it
- Basic programming concepts through sports statistics
- How to write their first lines of code
- Calculating personal performance metrics (averages, percentages, trends)

**Key Activities:**
- Setting up Google Colab coding environment
- Writing code to calculate shooting percentages and game averages
- Creating personal stat trackers using their own athletic data

**Engagement Hook:**  
Students see immediate relevance by calculating their own performance stats in the first session.

---

### **Week 2: Working with Real Sports Data**
**Sessions 3-4 | Professional-Level Analysis**

**What Students Learn:**
- How to open and explore large datasets (like those used by NBA scouts)
- Sorting and filtering data to find top performers
- Creating custom rankings and statistics
- Thinking like a talent scout using numbers

**Key Activities:**
- Loading real NBA player statistics (450+ professional athletes)
- Finding hidden talent using data filters
- Comparing favorite players across multiple metrics
- Identifying underrated players using statistical evidence

**Engagement Hook:**  
Students work with the exact same data that professional scouts and analysts use, analyzing players they watch on TV.

---

### **Week 3: Data Visualization**
**Sessions 5-6 | Making Data Beautiful**

**What Students Learn:**
- Creating professional-quality charts and graphs
- Visualizing player comparisons and performance trends
- Designing multi-dimensional stat profiles
- Presenting data in compelling, easy-to-understand formats

**Key Activities:**
- Building bar charts comparing top NBA scorers
- Creating complete player profiles showing all-around performance
- Tracking personal improvement over time with line graphs
- Designing custom visualizations with colors and styles

**Engagement Hook:**  
Students create ESPN-style graphics and can visualize their own performance alongside professional athletes.

---

### **Week 4: Finding Patterns & Correlations**
**Sessions 7-8 | Discovery Through Data**

**What Students Learn:**
- How to identify relationships between different statistics
- Understanding correlation (do two factors relate to each other?)
- Critical thinking about causation vs. correlation
- Using scatter plots to visualize patterns

**Key Activities:**
- Investigating questions like "Do taller players score more points?"
- Calculating correlation coefficients to measure relationship strength
- Exploring surprising connections in NBA data
- Applying correlation analysis to their own sport

**Engagement Hook:**  
Students become data detectives, uncovering hidden patterns that explain what makes athletes successful.

---

### **Week 5: Independent Project Work**
**Sessions 9-10 | Becoming the Analyst**

**What Students Learn:**
- Planning and executing an independent data analysis project
- Applying all previously learned skills in an integrated way
- Troubleshooting and problem-solving independently
- Preparing professional presentations

**Project Options (Students choose one):**

**Option A: Player Comparison Report**  
Compare 3-5 professional athletes across multiple statistics and determine who performs best

**Option B: Team Performance Analysis**  
Analyze what factors predict whether a team wins or loses games

**Option C: Personal Performance Tracker**  
Use their own athletic data to identify what training approaches improve their game

**Engagement Hook:**  
Students take ownership of their learning by investigating sports questions they genuinely care about.

---

### **Week 6: Presentations & Career Pathways**
**Sessions 11-12 | Showcase & Future Opportunities**

**What Students Learn:**
- Communicating technical findings to non-technical audiences
- Presenting data-driven insights with confidence
- Understanding career opportunities in sports analytics and technology
- Next steps for continued learning and skill development

**Key Activities:**
- Student presentations (3 minutes each with peer feedback)
- Career pathway discussions featuring real job opportunities
- Guest speaker from sports analytics field (if available)
- Certificate ceremony and program celebration

**Career Pathways Explored:**
- Sports Data Analyst (teams, media companies)
- Performance Coach (using data to optimize training)
- Sports Technology Entrepreneur (building apps and tools)
- Scout/Recruiter (data-driven talent identification)
- Sports Journalist (data-driven storytelling)

**Engagement Hook:**  
Students see tangible career opportunities that combine their passion for sports with valuable technical skills.

## **Program Differentiators**

**1. Sports-First Approach**  
Every coding concept is introduced through sports contexts that students already care about, making abstract programming concrete and relevant.

**2. Zero Barriers to Entry**  
Google Colab requires no software installation or technical setup, making the program accessible to all students regardless of their technology access at home.

**3. Immediate Relevance**  
Students analyze real professional athlete data and their own performance statistics, seeing immediate applications for their learning.

**4. Culturally Responsive Design**  
The curriculum features diverse athlete examples, honors different sports beyond traditional mainstream coverage, and connects to students' lived experiences.

**5. Growth-Oriented Assessment**  
Focus on improvement and effort rather than technical perfection, creating a safe environment for beginners to take risks and learn from mistakes.

---

## **Accessibility & Inclusion**

This program is designed specifically for marginalized high school athletes who may not have had prior exposure to coding or STEM education. Key inclusive features:

- **No prerequisites:** Assumes zero coding experience
- **Culturally relevant:** Uses sports students know and care about
- **Multiple entry points:** Offers different project options to match interests
- **Peer support built-in:** Emphasizes collaboration over competition
- **Growth mindset:** Celebrates mistakes as learning opportunities
- **Flexible pacing:** Allows advanced students to explore while supporting struggling learners

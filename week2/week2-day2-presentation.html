Filtering: Find What You Want üîç

Filtering = Finding rows that meet specific conditions

Like searching for players who score over 20 PPG!

Basic Filtering Examples

# Find players who score over 25 PPG
high_scorers = data[data['PTS'] > 25]
print(high_scorers[['Player', 'Team', 'PTS']])
# Find players on the Lakers
lakers = data[data['Team'] == 'LAL']
print(lakers[['Player', 'PTS']])
# Find players with over 10 rebounds
rebounders = data[data['REB'] > 10]
print(rebounders[['Player', 'REB']])

Multiple Conditions üîó

Combine filters with AND / OR

# Players who score 20+ AND rebound 8+
all_around = data[(data['PTS'] > 20) & (data['REB'] > 8)]
print(all_around[['Player', 'PTS', 'REB']])
# Players who score 30+ OR assist 10+
elite = data[(data['PTS'] > 30) | (data['AST'] > 10)]
print(elite[['Player', 'PTS', 'AST']])
# Lakers players who score over 15
lakers_scorers = data[(data['Team'] == 'LAL') & (data['PTS'] > 15)]

Important Symbols! ‚ö†Ô∏è

&

AND

Both conditions must be true

|

OR

At least one condition must be true

üí° Always use ( ) around each condition!

Sorting Data ‚¨ÜÔ∏è‚¨áÔ∏è

Find the TOP performers!

# Sort by points (lowest to highest)
sorted_pts = data.sort_values('PTS')
# Sort by points (highest to lowest)
top_scorers = data.sort_values('PTS', ascending=False)

# Top 10 rebounders
top_rebounders = data.sort_values('REB', ascending=False)
print(top_rebounders[['Player', 'REB']].head(10))

Putting It All Together! üéØ

# Complex query: Top 5 Lakers scorers
# Step 1: Filter for Lakers

What Makes a Good Question? üí°

‚úÖ Specific: Not too broad

‚úÖ Answerable: You can use data

‚úÖ Interesting: YOU care about it

‚úÖ Analytical: Requires comparison/calculation

Good vs Bad Questions

‚ùå Too Vague

"Who is the best player?"

(What does "best" mean?)


"What makes teams good?"

(Too broad!)

‚úÖ Much Better!

"Who is the most efficient scorer?"

(Use TS% - measurable!)


"Is scoring or defense more important?"

(Can test with data!)

Project Option 1 üèÄ

Player Comparison Analysis

Question: "Who's better: Player A vs B vs C?"

What you'll do:

  • Compare 3-5 players
  • Use multiple metrics
  • Create bar charts & scatter plots
  • Write conclusion

Perfect if you love specific players!

Project Option 2 üèÜ

Team Performance Analysis

Question: "What makes [Team] successful?"

What you'll do:

  • Analyze one team deeply
  • Compare to league average
  • Find strengths & weaknesses
  • Make recommendations

Perfect if you follow a specific team!

Project Option 3 üíé

Find the Hidden Gem

Question: "Who's the most underrated player?"

What you'll do:

  • Find efficient players with low minutes
  • Use advanced metrics
  • Create scatter plot
  • Argue why they should play more

Perfect if you want to discover something new!

Your Project Plan Template üìù

1. Research Question: What are you investigating?

2. Hypothesis: What do you think you'll find?

3. Data Needed: Which players? Which stats?

4. Analysis Plan: What calculations?

5. Visualizations: What charts? (at least 2)

6. Challenges: What might be hard?

Example Research Question üí°

Question:

"Which high-usage players are most efficient?"

Hypothesis:

"I think superstars with high usage will have lower efficiency because they take harder shots."

Data Needed:

Players with USG% > 25%, their TS% and PTS

Visualizations:

Scatter plot (USG% vs TS%) + Bar chart (Top 5 efficient high-usage players)
